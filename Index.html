Strategic Insights & Qualitative Review

Top 3 Narrative Gaps

Gap 1: "Eco-Friendly & Recyclable Packaging"

AI responses to queries about sustainable oral care rarely highlight any single brand as a clear leader. While some mention pilot programs, no brand has "owned" the narrative for fully recyclable toothpaste tubes, plastic-free floss, or biodegradable toothbrush heads. This is a significant open opportunity.

Gap 2: "Advanced Gum Health & Gingivitis Reversal"

For queries beyond basic cleaning (e.g., "best toothpaste for receding gums"), AI answers are fragmented. They often mention active ingredients like Stannous Fluoride but fail to consistently associate one brand as the definitive solution for actively improving gum health.

Gap 3: "Solutions for Cosmetic Dentistry & Post-Procedure Care"

AIs currently provide generic advice for users with veneers, bonding, or implants. There is no brand consistently recommended for "safe whitening for bonding" or "best toothpaste for implants." This specialized, high-value narrative is unclaimed.

Top 3 Competitor Threats

Threat 1: Sensodyne's Dominance in "Sensitivity"

Sensodyne has fortified its position as the undisputed leader for "sensitive teeth." In 85% of relevant queries, it's the top recommendation. Our brand's sensitivity products are increasingly being positioned by AI as a secondary alternative rather than a direct peer.

Threat 2: Crest's Aggressive Push in "Whitening"

Crest 3D White is showing new strength in "whitening" queries. AI models are citing new clinical studies and influencer reviews favoring Crest, leading to a 5-point increase in their Mention Share for commercial investigation prompts this quarter.

Threat 3: Listerine's Expansion into "Total Oral Health"

Listerine is successfully expanding its narrative beyond "bad breath." AI responses are increasingly citing their products for "killing plaque germs" and as a crucial step in a "dentist-approved routine," directly challenging the "whole mouth health" narrative.

Emerging Sources

Source 1: TikTok "Dentist-Influencers"

AI models are beginning to summarize and cite short-form video content from popular dental professionals on TikTok and Instagram Reels. They are being used as sources for product "hacks" and "what dentists actually use."

Source 2: Scientific Pre-Print Servers (e.g., medRxiv)

For queries about ingredient efficacy, AIs are now citing pre-print medical and dental studies. This indicates a shift towards sourcing data before it's gone through full peer review, prioritizing novelty.

Source 3: Niche Health & Wellness Blogs

Small, independent wellness blogs focusing on "non-toxic living" or "holistic health" are emerging as sources for "fluoride-free" and "SLS-free" product recommendations, influencing the narrative in this growing sub-category.

Glossary

Metric NameFramework PillarDefinitionFormula/MethodologyStrategic Importance
Retrieval Share (RS)VisibilityThe percentage of times a brand appears in search results compared to its competitors.(Brand Appearances / Total Appearances) * 100Indicates brand visibility and search engine optimization effectiveness.
Mention Share (MS)VisibilityThe percentage of times a brand is mentioned in online content compared to its competitors.(Brand Mentions / Total Mentions) * 100Reflects brand awareness and media presence.
Narrative Share (NS)VisibilityThe percentage of times a brand's narrative is dominant in online discussions compared to its competitors.(Brand Narrative Dominance / Total Narrative Dominance) * 100Measures the strength and prevalence of a brand's key messages.
Comparative Position Score (CPS)PositioningA score that reflects a brand's position relative to its competitors based on key attributes.Weighted average of attribute scoresProvides a comprehensive view of brand positioning and competitive advantage.
Sentiment Valence Score (SVS)PositioningA score that measures the overall sentiment (positive, negative, neutral) associated with a brand compared to its competitors.Average sentiment score across mentionsIndicates brand perception and emotional resonance.
Citation Rate (CR)AdoptionThe frequency with which a brand's data or insights are cited in external sources.(Brand Citations / Total Citations) * 100Reflects the credibility and influence of a brand's data.
Data Adoption Rate (DAR)AdoptionThe percentage of users or organizations that have adopted a brand's data or insights.(Users Adopting Brand Data / Total Users) * 100Measures the practical application and utility of a brand's data.
Citation Click-Through Rate (cCTR)AdoptionThe percentage of clicks on citations of a brand's data or insights.(Clicks on Brand Citations / Total Clicks on Citations) * 100Indicates user engagement and interest in a brand's data.
AI-Influenced Conversion Rate (AICR)AdoptionThe percentage of conversions attributed to AI-driven insights or recommendations from a brand.(Conversions from AI Insights / Total Conversions) * 100Measures the impact of AI on driving business outcomes.
